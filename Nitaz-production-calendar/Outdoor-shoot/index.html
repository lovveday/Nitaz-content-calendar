Black dress · Video 1 · Rebuilt · 90–120 seconds
"The dinner"
Opens inside the restaurant on the prop couple — sweet words, a private moment. Then outside, Nitaz steps out of a car. The street stops. She walks in. The room shifts. And by the end, even the prop wife cannot pretend she is not impressed.
Anniversary dinner Hotel restaurant — outside + inside Layered ending Funny + romantic + brand
Scene 1 — Opening: the prop couple inside
Scene 1 Sweet words — the setup Restaurant interior — prop couple's table 0:00 – 0:18
1 Drift in The camera drifts slowly into the restaurant — past tables, past soft light, until it finds the prop couple seated together. They are in their own world. He is leaning forward, talking to her quietly.
The opening has no urgency. It is warm and intimate. We are entering a world, not starting an action.
 Slow push-in or glide shot from the restaurant entrance moving toward their table. Like we are walking in and finding them.
2 Medium The prop man, eyes on his wife, saying something sweet. He reaches across and takes her hand.
We do not hear the words but we feel them. "You are the most beautiful woman in the world" energy. She smiles — she believes him completely.
 This moment is the irony that makes the whole film work — he is saying she is the most beautiful, and the universe is about to test that statement. Plant it clearly.
3 Close-up Her face — warm, happy, a little shy. She squeezes his hand. She believes every word.
Hold for 2 seconds. This is the emotional anchor we come back to later.
 Warm light on her face. She should look genuinely touched. This moment needs to be sweet enough that the audience likes her before the comedy hits.
Scene 2 — Cut outside: the restaurant exterior
Scene 2 Where are we — establishing the world Restaurant exterior — outside 0:18 – 0:28
4 Wide Cut outside. The restaurant exterior — the building, the entrance, a couple of people outside. The world outside the restaurant. Small activity — a person on their phone, two people chatting near the entrance.
This shot tells the audience where we are and sets up the arrival. It should feel like a normal, calm evening outside a nice restaurant.
 Wide enough to see the full entrance and at least 2–3 people outside naturally positioned. Do not make them look like they are waiting — they are just existing.
5 Cut back Brief cut back inside — the prop man still talking, still sweet. The wife laughing softly at something he said.
One last beat of their sweetness before everything changes.
 3 seconds maximum. Just enough to remind us they are there and they are happy. Then cut back outside for the arrival.
Scene 3 — The arrival: she steps out of the car
Scene 3 She arrives Restaurant exterior — car arrival 0:28 – 0:48
6 Wide A car pulls up to the restaurant entrance. The door opens.
A beat of stillness. Then — her shoes touch the ground first. We see nothing else yet. Just the shoes stepping out onto the pavement.
 Shoot from low angle. The shoes hitting the ground is the first reveal of Nitaz. It builds anticipation. KEY SHOT
7 Low → Wide The camera rises slowly — from the shoes, up the dress, revealing the full look as she stands and straightens. The cup skirt. The embellishment. Her face last.
She does not look at the camera. She looks straight ahead — toward the restaurant entrance. She knows where she is going.
 This rising reveal shot is the visual centrepiece of the whole film. Do multiple takes. Start tight on the shoes, rise slowly, end on her face. Slow motion on the rise. KEY SHOT
8 Cut sequence The people outside — cut between 2–3 quick reaction shots. The person on their phone lowers it. The two people mid-conversation stop talking mid-sentence. A beat of silence before they all just stare.
No exaggeration. Just the natural human reaction to seeing something beautiful. One second each.
 Three cuts, one second each, fast. The edit creates the sense that everyone stopped at once. Then cut to her walking.
9 Tracking Nitaz begins walking toward the restaurant entrance. The camera tracks alongside her or slightly ahead — capturing her full stride, the cup skirt moving with each step.
Unhurried. Confident. She is not performing — she is simply going to dinner.
 Track from the side so the silhouette of the cup skirt is visible. The movement of this dress walking is a visual asset — use it fully.
Scene 4 — The walk inside: angles, details, the room
Scene 4 Inside the restaurant — the full showcase Restaurant interior — entrance through to the floor 0:48 – 1:15
10 Through glass Shot from inside the restaurant — through the glass door or window, we see Nitaz approaching from outside. She pushes the door open and steps in.
The inside world sees her before she fully arrives. The camera is waiting for her.
 This transition from outside to inside is cinematic and intentional. The audience feels like they are inside the restaurant watching her arrive. KEY SHOT
11 Close-up Her shoes on the restaurant floor — the sound of heels, the dress hem just above the knee, the embellishment catching the interior light.
Every step is deliberate. The shoes and the dress hem together in one frame.
 Low angle. This is the shot that connects the exterior walk to the interior walk. The shoes are a continuous thread through the whole arrival sequence.
12 Close-up The dress detail — embellishment pattern, fabric texture, the design as she moves. The camera finds specific details: a cluster of embellishment, the hem, the waist.
Three or four detail inserts cut together quickly — 1 second each. The dress gets its full showcase here.
 Have a list of specific dress details to capture before the shoot. Each one gets its own 1-second insert. These details are what clients are watching for.
13 Side angle The critical angle — Nitaz walking in the foreground, the prop couple visible in the background at their table. Both in frame. She does not know they are watching. They do not know she is the reason the room shifted.
This single frame connects the two storylines — her walk and their reaction — without cutting away.
 This is a composition shot — it requires precise blocking. Nitaz walks left to right in the foreground. The prop couple must be positioned so they are visible but slightly out of focus in the background. Rehearse this specific angle before the shoot. KEY SHOT
14 Cut to couple The prop man's eyes drift from his wife toward Nitaz mid-sentence. He stops talking. The wife is still speaking — she has not noticed yet.
He is not being dramatic. His eyes just go. He cannot help it.
 Play this subtly. The comedy is in the involuntary nature of it — he is not trying to look, he just does.
15 Close-up The wife mid-sentence, realising she has lost him. She follows his gaze. Then — she slaps his arm to get his attention back. Quick, sharp, clear.
He snaps back. Innocent face. She gives him a look. He picks up his glass.
 The arm slap lands harder than a hand slap and reads more naturally. Hold one extra beat after the slap for the audience to react.
16 Medium A second couple at another table — a woman notices Nitaz, leans toward her companion and whispers something. Her companion looks up and nods. A shared moment of admiration.
This widens the room's reaction beyond just the prop couple. The whole restaurant is quietly aware of her.
 Keep this brief — 2 seconds. It is a supporting reaction, not a featured one. The prop couple is the comedy. This couple is the elegance.
17 From behind Shot from behind Nitaz's husband — his back to camera, standing, watching his wife walk toward him. Nitaz in full frame ahead of him, closing the distance. The restaurant behind her.
We see what he sees. We feel what he feels.
 This is a point-of-view shot — we are the husband. The audience falls a little in love with her through his eyes. KEY SHOT
18 Close-up His face — the slow smile of a man completely in love. Eyes fixed on her, not blinking. The kind of look that says he has seen her a hundred times and she still does this to him every single time.
Hold on his face for 3 full seconds. Do not cut away too early.
 This is one of the most important shots in the film — a man looking at his wife like that is one of the most universally moving images. Cast this look carefully.
Scene 5 — She arrives at his side
Scene 5 She reaches him Their table 1:15 – 1:28
19 Medium Nitaz reaches her husband. He steps forward — not to hug immediately, just to be close. He looks at her like he is seeing her for the first time.
He says something — we do not hear it. She smiles in response. Whatever he said was the right thing.
 This moment is quiet and private — the restaurant carries on around them but the camera is only interested in them.
20 Close-up He pulls out her chair. She sits. The cup skirt settles perfectly as she sits — the dress has one final moment of beauty before the scene changes.
Her hands smooth the dress as she sits. Deliberate and graceful.
 Capture the dress sitting down — the cup shape settling is a beautiful image and a last showcase of the silhouette.
Scene 6 — The layered ending (three beats)
Ending beat 1 The funny callback — even she cannot resist 1:28 – 1:40
21 Cut to couple Cut back to the prop couple. The man is being very well-behaved now — eyes forward, talking to his wife, overcompensating slightly.
Then the wife's eyes drift. She looks across at Nitaz seated at the table. She stares — admiring the dress. She cannot help it either.
 Mirror the earlier beat exactly — but now it is her. The audience sees the symmetry and laughs.
22 Close-up The prop husband notices his wife staring this time. He raises an eyebrow at her — a slow, knowing look. She catches herself and snaps back.
They look at each other. A beat. Then they both laugh — at themselves, at the whole situation.
 This is the release. The audience has been waiting for this reversal since the arm slap. Let the laughter breathe for 2 seconds before cutting.
Ending beat 2 The romantic close — the real message 1:40 – 1:52
23 Medium Back to Nitaz and her husband at their table. He leans forward and says something quietly in her ear. She closes her eyes briefly — whatever he said landed.
She opens her eyes and smiles — the private smile of a woman who feels completely seen.
 We do not hear what he says. It does not matter. The audience will project their own version of it. That is the point.
24 Wide pull-back The camera pulls back slowly — wide shot of the full restaurant. Their table in frame. The prop couple in frame. The second couple in frame. Life carrying on. And at the centre of it all, quietly, Nitaz.
Hold this wide shot for 4 full seconds. No cuts. Just the room.
 This is the most important frame in the film. Everything — the comedy, the romance, the admiration — all visible simultaneously. Take time to get this composition right. KEY SHOT
Ending beat 3 The brand — the last word 1:52 – 2:00
25 Close-up One last close-up — the embellishment detail on the black dress. The same shot that opened the film, mirroring the beginning.
The film began with the dress. It ends with the dress. Everything in between is what this dress does to a room.
 Cut to black directly from the embellishment close-up. Then white text: "Timeless elegance." Hold. Then: "@nitazofficial_"

Black dress · Video 2 · Two alternate directions
"The birthday entrance" — choose your direction
Same dress, same occasion — two completely different ways to tell the story. Direction A is cinematic and emotional. Direction B is fun, social, and built for maximum shareability. Or merge them.
DIRECTION A
"She came. She turned 40. She conquered."
A solo cinematic film. No friends, no big group. Just Nitaz, the hotel, and the moment. The emotional weight comes from what turning 40 means — not from the party around her.
Tone Cinematic. Introspective. The voiceover or on-screen text carries the emotional story. The visuals are almost silent.
Direction A — Scene by scene
Scene 1The numberHotel room0:00 – 0:12
1Close-up A birthday card or phone notification — "Happy 40th." Her hand sets it down. She stands up.
The number 40 is the entire context. No other explanation needed.
 The number does the emotional work. Every person watching has a milestone they are approaching or have passed.
2On-screen text Text appears over black: "40 years. Every one of them earned."
Hold for 2 seconds. Then cut to her walking out of the hotel room in the black dress — full look, fully ready.
 White text, black background. Simple font. The words should feel like a quiet declaration, not a celebration slogan.
Scene 2The arrival — she enters her own party aloneHotel staircase or entrance0:12 – 0:40
3Low angle Nitaz descends a hotel staircase — shot from the bottom looking up. Full black dress, cup skirt visible from below, embellishment catching the staircase light with each step.
Slow. Every step is intentional. She is not going down stairs. She is making an entrance.
 The staircase shot is the signature image of this direction. If the hotel has a grand staircase, use it. This is the one visual people will screenshot. KEY SHOT
4Close-up Her hand on the stair rail. Her face — composed, a quiet smile. She is not nervous. She is ready.
Two inserts: hand on rail, then her face. Together they say: I have been waiting for this.
 Warm staircase light on her face if possible. This close-up should feel intimate and private.
Scene 3The reactions and the partyParty space0:40 – 1:05
5Wide She enters the party space. The room reacts — but slowly, like a ripple. One person sees her, then the next, then the next.
Three reaction shots in sequence — admiring, respectful, warm. Not shock — recognition. They see a woman in her full power.
 This direction's reactions are quieter than Direction B. No jumping, no screaming. Just the universal human response to witnessing something truly beautiful.
6Wide pull-back Final wide shot — Nitaz in the party, the room around her. On-screen text fades in over the image: "The most beautiful woman in the room. Every time."
Hold for 4 seconds. Cut to brand end card.
 The text is the ending. It connects the film back to Nitaz's brand mission and makes the message explicit without anyone having to say a word.
DIRECTION B
"Nobody was ready for her."
Fun, energetic, social. This is the birthday video people tag their friends in. It opens with the friends already at the party hyping each other up — waiting, almost nervous on her behalf. Then she arrives and chaos (the good kind) breaks out.
Tone Fun and warm. More energy. Reactions are bigger. Think the feeling of watching a real birthday entrance video that goes viral — but elevated.
Direction B — Scene by scene
Scene 1The friends are waiting — and they are excitedParty space0:00 – 0:15
1Medium Two or three friends at the party — one checks her phone, shows the screen to the others. They all laugh and look toward the entrance with anticipation.
We do not see the screen. We just know something is coming and they cannot wait.
 The friends are the hook. Their excitement transfers to the audience. We are now waiting with them.
Scene 2The entrance — nobody was readyParty entrance0:15 – 0:40
2Wide Nitaz appears in the doorway. Full stop. Black dress, embellishment, cup silhouette. She pauses — takes in the room — then walks in.
The friends see her first. One of them physically grabs the arm of another.
 The arm grab is the comedy beat of this direction — involuntary, instinctive, real. It is more relatable than the prop couple hand slap and works for a birthday energy. KEY SHOT
3Slow-mo Slow motion — Nitaz walks into the party. The cup skirt moves. The embellishment catches the party light. Her face is calm amidst all the reaction around her.
She is unbothered. This is just Tuesday for a Nitaz woman.
 The contrast between her calm and everyone else's reaction is the comedic and aspirational core of this direction.
Scene 3The reactions — full energyParty space0:40 – 1:05
4Cut sequence Fast cuts — five reactions in five seconds: (a) a woman's jaw drops. (b) someone points. (c) a man does a double take. (d) a friend rushes toward her. (e) someone points their phone at her.
One second each. The edit is the comedy — rapid fire reactions create the feeling that the whole room exploded at once.
 Brief these five people specifically on what they are doing. One second reactions require precise timing — do a rehearsal pass before shooting.
5Medium The friend reaches Nitaz — huge hug, pulls back, looks at the dress, grabs her shoulders, says something like "this dress!" We do not hear it but we read it.
The friend's reaction is the surrogate for every viewer who wants to be at that party.
 Direction B ends on joy — wide shot, Nitaz laughing with her friend, the party around her fully alive. Then cut to brand end card.
Merge idea Open with Direction B (friends waiting, energetic entrance, big reactions) then transition into Direction A's ending — on-screen text "The most beautiful woman in the room. Every time." The energy of B with the brand weight of A. Strong combination.
Black dress · Video 3 · Two alternate directions
"Getting ready" — choose your direction
The same ritual — a woman preparing for her moment. Two completely different emotional angles. Direction A is quiet and introspective. Direction B adds a second character that reframes the whole story.
DIRECTION A — original
"The ritual" — solo, silent, intentional
Just Nitaz and the dress. No other characters. The getting-ready process as a meditation — each action deliberate, each detail captured. The occasion is never named. The dress is the only clue.
Direction A — Scene by scene
Scene 1The dress before the womanHotel room0:00 – 0:15
1Close-up The black dress laid flat on the hotel bed — cup skirt visible, embellishment catching the light. Nobody in frame. The dress is waiting.
Still shot. 3 seconds. The scroll-stopper is the question: whose dress is this, and where are they going?
 Style this shot carefully. The dress should look like it belongs in a magazine. Smooth the fabric, let the embellishment catch the light, keep the background clean.
Scene 2The small ritualsHotel room dressing table0:15 – 0:38
2Close-up series A sequence of close-up hands-only shots — perfume, earrings going in, a necklace being fastened, lipstick.
Four cuts, 2–3 seconds each. No face yet. Just hands and rituals.
 These shots build anticipation. Every woman watching recognises this ritual. It creates intimacy before the reveal.
Scene 3She puts on the dressHotel room0:38 – 0:58
3From behind Shot from behind — she steps into the dress, pulls it up, the zip closes. The shape of the dress forms around her.
Her hands smooth the cup skirt into place. The silhouette is complete.
 If the back of the dress has detail, this is where it lives. If the zip is decorative, linger on it closing.
Scene 4The mirror — the full revealHotel room mirror0:58 – 1:15
4Medium She turns to the full-length mirror. For the first time in the film — the complete look. Face, dress, shoes. Everything.
She looks at herself. Not vanity. Satisfaction. A quiet nod. She is ready.
 Hold this for 4 seconds. This is the reveal that the entire film has been building toward. Do not cut away too soon. KEY SHOT
Scene 5She leaves — and the room waitsHotel corridor1:15 – 1:28
5Tracking She walks out the door into the corridor. Tracking shot from behind — the dress moving as she walks away. The lift button is pressed. Doors open. She steps in. They close.
Cut to black the moment the doors close. Then the brand end card.
 The closed lift doors are the ending. The occasion is about to happen. We do not follow her — we stay behind. That restraint is what makes the ending hit.
DIRECTION B — alternate
"The phone call" — getting ready with a best friend on the line
She is getting ready and her best friend is on a video call — watching, hyping her up, reacting to the dress in real time. The friend's reactions from the phone screen become the audience surrogate. Warmer, funnier, more relatable.
Tone Warm and fun. The friend on the call is the comedy and the emotion. Her reactions tell the audience exactly how to feel about the dress.

The friend: "Okay show me the dress. Show me now." Nitaz: she smiles and points toward the dress on the bed.
 The friend on the phone is the hook. The audience immediately wants to see the dress because the friend does.

 The friend's reaction to the dress is the first audience surrogate moment. Her response sets the emotional temperature for the whole video. KEY SHOT

 The friend standing up involuntarily is the big comedy beat of this direction. It is instinctive and real and the audience will love it.

 Ending the call is the transition from private to public. From getting ready to being ready. The corridor shot seals it.
