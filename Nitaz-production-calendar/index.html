Shoot Day 1 — Thursday, Week 1
This shoot feeds Weeks 1 & 2 content. Plan for approx. 4–5 hours. Have dresses pressed and ready before the day.
Finished dresses to shoot
1Birthday gown — full body shots, detail closeups, fabric texture. 6–8 usable frames for carousel.IG
2Owambe gown — full look, movement shots if possible, bold silhouette angles.Both
3Third dress (any current finished piece) — at minimum 4 clean shots for future carousel.IG
Video content to capture
1Designer talking to camera — "she turned 40 and wanted to own the room." 60–90 sec, one take, natural.TikTok
2Sketch process — pencil on paper, close-up, no talking needed. 30–60 sec raw footage.TikTok
3BTS — rack of clothes, hands at work, fabric being handled. Silent or with ambient sound.IG
4Birthday gown reveal — slow pan or transition-style reveal video for TikTok POV post.Both
Talking-to-camera scripts
1"She turned 40 and told me she wanted to walk in and feel like the room stopped." — open this way, tell the story.
2"Why does your birthday deserve a custom dress?" — opinion piece, 45 sec max, direct to lens.
3"How Nitaz started" — founder story, keep it under 90 sec, personal and honest.
Shoot Day 2 — Thursday, Week 3
This shoot feeds Weeks 3 & 4 content. Focus on emotional and brand-identity material. Also grab month recap footage.
Finished dresses to shoot
1The month's best dress — this is the hero piece for Week 4 carousel. Treat this as editorial. Best lighting, cleanest background.IG
2Graduation or gala dress — full body + detail shots. Occasion framing in caption.IG
3Original sketch next to finished dress — flat lay or side-by-side setup. One clean shot is enough.Both
Video content to capture
1Month recap B-roll — hands sewing, dresses on rack, fabric rolls, the studio in motion. 2–3 min raw, edit down to 30 sec.IG
2Sketch-to-dress reveal — film the sketch, cut to the dress. Transition moment.Both
3Designer talking to cam — "timeless elegance" brand definition. Personal, unhurried.TikTok
Talking-to-camera scripts
1"A dress that made me cry" — pick a real story from a real client. Tell it plainly. Don't over-rehearse.
2"Nigerian women deserve better from their designers" — hot take, specific, not vague. State the problem clearly.
3"Timeless elegance is not about the trend. It is about the woman." — 45 sec brand statement.
